  • The initial deposit is made today.
  • Regular monthly deposits are made throughout the year. These are summed up annually for the purpose of interest calculation.
  • Interest is credited annually, after the full year's contributions have been summed up.

The formula used for compound interest is:

\[ C = C_0 \times (1 + r) + I \times \left(\frac{(1 + r)^1 - 1}{r}\right) \]

Where:

  • C = Final capital (total value at the end of the period)
  • C0 = Initial capital (starting amount)
  • r = Rate of return (annual interest rate, expressed as a decimal, e.g., 10% → 0.10)
  • I = Total annual contribution (monthly savings × 12)
  • (1 + r) = Growth factor applied to the total capital, including initial capital and contributions
  • \(\frac{(1 + r)^1 - 1}{r}\) = Factor for calculating the compounded interest on annual contributions

How to Calculate Your Interest Over Time

  • Starting Balance: The amount you currently have in savings.
  • Monthly Contributions: Additional savings you plan to deposit each month.
  • Investment Horizon: The number of years you plan to keep your money invested.
  • Rate of Return: The expected annual return on your investment (typically around 10%).
  • Annual Costs: Any fees, such as broker charges or investment management costs.
